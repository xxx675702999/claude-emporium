## Progressive Examples

### Example 1: Magic System Rules

The world has a rule: magic costs physical stamina. The more powerful the spell, the greater the exhaustion.

**Draft (exposition dump):**

> **zh**: 这个世界的魔法以施法者的体力为代价。低级法术只需要消耗少量体力，但高级法术可能会让施法者筋疲力尽，严重时甚至会导致昏迷或死亡。因此，法师们通常需要保持良好的身体素质。
> **en**: Magic in this world costs physical stamina. Low-level spells consume only a little, but advanced spells can exhaust the caster to the point of collapse or death. For this reason, mages typically maintain peak physical fitness.

**Revision 1 (Layer 1 — embedded in action):**

> **zh**: 她念完咒语，火球从掌心飞出去，把那棵枯树烧成了灰。她弯下腰，双手撑着膝盖喘了十几秒。"没事，"她说。"小法术而已。"但她的手在抖。
> **en**: She finished the incantation. The fireball left her palm and burned the dead tree to ash. She bent over, hands on her knees, gasping for ten seconds. "I'm fine," she said. "It was a small spell." But her hands were trembling.

**Revision 2 (Layer 2 — rules clarified through character interaction):**

> **zh**: "你怎么喘成这样？"他问。"不就一个火球吗？"
> "你试试连着放三个。"她从包里翻出一块干粮塞进嘴里。"体力跟不上的话，第四个能把人放晕过去。"
> "那你刚才是第几个？"
> 她没回答，又吃了一块。
>
> **en**: "Why are you breathing like that?" he asked. "It was just a fireball."
> "You try casting three in a row." She dug a ration bar from her pack and shoved it in her mouth. "Run out of stamina and the fourth one can knock you out cold."
> "How many was that for you?"
> She didn't answer and ate another bar.

**Revision 3 (Layer 3 — deep rule revealed at crisis moment):**

> **zh**: 第七个火球打出去的时候，她的鼻子开始流血。他喊她停手，她没听见——或者听见了。第八个火球比前面的都小，飞出去三尺就散了。她倒下来的时候他接住了她。她的体温在下降，像是被什么东西从里往外抽走了热量。"她在燃烧自己的生命力，"老法师说。"超过极限之后，法术不再消耗体力——它消耗寿命。"
> **en**: When the seventh fireball left her hand, her nose started bleeding. He shouted at her to stop. She didn't hear — or did. The eighth fireball was smaller than the others, dissolving three feet out. When she collapsed he caught her. Her body temperature was dropping, as if something was draining heat from the inside out. "She's burning her own life force," the old mage said. "Past the limit, spells stop costing stamina. They cost years."

**Why the progression works**: Layer 1 shows the rule through physical consequence — the reader sees that magic is exhausting. Layer 2 uses dialogue to quantify the limit — "three in a row" — and the unanswered question implies she's been pushing past it. Layer 3 reveals the ultimate cost at a crisis point, making the rule feel like a discovery rather than a lecture.

### Example 2: Political Backstory

The world has a history: two kingdoms fought a war twenty years ago. The current peace is fragile.

**Draft (history textbook):**

> **zh**: 二十年前，东晋与西凉爆发了一场持续三年的战争。战争以东晋的惨胜告终，双方签订了《云台条约》。条约规定西凉每年向东晋进贡，但西凉国内一直存在不满情绪。
> **en**: Twenty years ago, East Jin and West Liang fought a three-year war. East Jin won at great cost, and both sides signed the Yuntai Treaty, requiring annual tribute from West Liang. Resentment in West Liang has persisted.

**Revision 1 (Layer 1 — history through a scar):**

> **zh**: 客栈老板端酒过来的时候，他注意到老板的左手少了两根指头。"当年打西凉的时候丢的，"老板看见他在看，笑了一下。"算便宜的了。同一排的弟兄，回来的不到一半。"
> **en**: When the innkeeper brought the wine, he noticed the man was missing two fingers on his left hand. "Lost them in the Liang campaign," the innkeeper said, noticing his gaze with a grin. "Got off cheap. Less than half my unit made it back."

**Revision 2 (Layer 2 — treaty implications through daily life):**

> **zh**: 路过边境关卡的时候，西凉那边的商人被翻了三遍行李。"贡品不够数，"守关的兵说。"上头说了，今年查严点。"
> 一个西凉商人嘟囔了一句什么，守关的兵转过头来："你再说一遍？"
> 商人低下头，不说话了。
>
> **en**: At the border checkpoint, merchants from West Liang had their goods searched three times over. "Tribute count's short," the guard said. "Orders from above — tighter checks this year."
> One merchant muttered something under his breath. The guard turned. "Say that again?"
> The merchant lowered his head and said nothing.

**Revision 3 (Layer 3 — the fragile peace breaks, at the worst moment):**

> **zh**: 他正在谈判桌上说到第三条的时候，门外传来马蹄声。信使冲进来，盔甲上有血。"西凉反了，"信使说。"三天前。云台关已经破了。"
> 对面的西凉使节站起来，脸上没有意外的表情——他早就知道了。
>
> **en**: He was mid-sentence on the third clause when hoofbeats sounded outside. A messenger burst in, blood on his armor. "Liang has broken the treaty," the messenger said. "Three days ago. Yuntai Pass has fallen."
> The Liang envoy across the table stood. His face showed no surprise — he'd known all along.

**Why the progression works**: Layer 1 embeds history in a veteran's body. Layer 2 shows the treaty's ongoing friction through a border encounter. Layer 3 delivers the political rupture at a critical narrative moment — and the envoy's unsurprised expression retroactively reframes everything the reader has seen.

### Example 3: Character Backstory

A character has a traumatic past: her father was a corrupt official who was executed when she was twelve.

**Draft (backstory dump):**

> **zh**: 林小雪的父亲是前任户部侍郎，因贪腐被处决时她才十二岁。从那以后，她一直靠自己生活，性格变得冷漠而独立。
> **en**: Lin Xiaoxue's father was the former Vice Minister of Revenue, executed for corruption when she was twelve. Since then, she'd been on her own, developing a cold and independent personality.

**Revision 1 (Layer 1 — hint through behavior):**

> **zh**: 饭桌上有人提到朝廷查贪的事，她放下筷子说吃饱了。其实碗里还有半碗饭。
> **en**: When someone at the table mentioned the court's anti-corruption campaign, she put down her chopsticks and said she was full. Her bowl was still half finished.

**Revision 2 (Layer 2 — backstory leaks through a telling detail):**

> **zh**: 他注意到她从不用正门进出任何地方。"为什么走后门？"他问过一次。她说习惯了。后来他听别人说起——她小时候家被抄的那天，是从后门跑出去的。
> **en**: He noticed she never used the front entrance of any building. "Why the back door?" he asked once. She said it was habit. Later, someone told him — the day her family's home was raided, she'd escaped through the back.

**Revision 3 (Layer 3 — full reveal at emotional breaking point):**

> **zh**: 他追到房顶的时候，她正站在边缘。不是要跳——她在看对面那栋楼。
> "那是你家？"他问。
> "以前是。"
> 楼早就被封了，门上贴着官府的封条，褪色了。
> "我十二岁那年站在那个窗口，"她说。"看着我爹被押出去。他回头看了我一眼。"她停了一下。"他笑了。他居然笑了。"
> 她擦了擦眼睛。"所以我不恨他贪。我恨他笑。"
>
> **en**: When he caught up to her on the rooftop, she was standing at the edge. Not about to jump — she was looking at the building across the way.
> "Your old home?" he asked.
> "Used to be."
> The building had been sealed long ago, government notices on the door, faded.
> "I was twelve, standing at that window," she said. "Watching them drag my father out. He looked back at me." She paused. "He smiled. He actually smiled."
> She wiped her eyes. "So I don't hate him for the corruption. I hate him for the smile."

**Why the progression works**: Layer 1 is a behavioral clue — she has a strong reaction to a certain topic. Layer 2 explains the habit through secondhand information, giving the reader enough to start piecing things together. Layer 3 delivers the full emotional truth at a moment of vulnerability, and the specific detail of the father's smile gives it a complexity that no backstory dump could achieve.

### Example 4: Social Hierarchy

The world has castes: "Marked" people (born with a visible brand) are treated as second-class citizens.

**Draft (worldbuilding essay):**

> **zh**: 在这个社会中，"烙印者"是指出生时身上就带有特殊印记的人。他们被视为下等公民，不能进入贵族区域，不能从事体面的职业，婚配也受到严格限制。
> **en**: In this society, "Marked" people are born with a visible brand. They are treated as second-class citizens, barred from noble districts, denied respectable professions, and subject to strict marriage restrictions.

**Revision 1 (Layer 1 — show through one interaction):**

> **zh**: 她走进店里的时候，掌柜的眼神先落在她脖子上那块灰色的印记上，然后才看她的脸。"后门买，"掌柜说。
> **en**: When she walked into the shop, the owner's eyes landed on the gray mark on her neck before they reached her face. "Back door," the owner said.

**Revision 2 (Layer 2 — rules revealed through violation):**

> **zh**: "你怎么进来的？"贵族区的巡逻兵拦住了她。
> "我有通行令。"她递过一张纸。
> 巡逻兵看了半天。"这是谁签的？"
> "王府的管事。我给他们家送药。"
> 巡逻兵把纸还给她。"送完就走。别让人看见你在街上晃。"
>
> **en**: "How'd you get in here?" The patrol guard in the noble district stopped her.
> "I have a pass." She handed over a paper.
> The guard studied it. "Who signed this?"
> "The Wang household steward. I'm delivering medicine."
> The guard returned the paper. "Deliver it and leave. Don't let anyone see you wandering the streets."

**Revision 3 (Layer 3 — the system's deepest cruelty revealed at personal cost):**

> **zh**: 他们站在官署门口。他把戒指递给她。"这是什么？"她问。
> "你知道这是什么。"
> 她看着戒指，然后看着他。"你知道不可能。"
> "谁说的？"
> "法律说的。"她把他的手合上，连戒指一起推回去。"烙印者和无印者不能通婚。你要是娶了我，你的烙印会在三天内出现。不是天生的——是官府烙上去的。你的名字从族谱里删掉。你的孩子，生出来就是烙印者。"她低下头。"我查过。"
>
> **en**: They stood in front of the magistrate's office. He held out the ring. "What's this?" she asked.
> "You know what it is."
> She looked at the ring, then at him. "You know it's impossible."
> "Says who?"
> "Says the law." She closed his hand over the ring and pushed it back. "A Marked and an Unmarked can't marry. If you marry me, your mark appears within three days. Not born with — branded by the state. Your name is struck from the family register. Your children are born Marked." She looked down. "I checked."

**Why the progression works**: Layer 1 shows discrimination through a wordless social reflex. Layer 2 reveals the rules of segregation through a tense but practical encounter. Layer 3 exposes the system's deepest mechanism — the way it perpetuates itself through marriage law — at the moment of maximum personal cost.

## Genre Variants

### Xuanhuan/Xianxia

Cultivation systems are the most exposition-prone element. Avoid "cultivation rank charts" — reveal ranks through encounters.

**Rank revelation through consequence, not chart:**

> **zh** ✗: 修炼境界分为：练气、筑基、金丹、元婴、化神、合体、大乘、渡劫。每个境界分初中后三期。
> **zh** ✓: 他一掌拍过去，对方连衣服都没动。"筑基？"对方打了个哈欠。"练气期的东西碰我一根毛都不够。"他说完转身走了。连杀的兴趣都没有。
>
> **en** ✗: Cultivation ranks are: Qi Condensation, Foundation Establishment, Core Formation, Nascent Soul, Spirit Severing, Void Union, Great Vehicle, Tribulation Transcendence.
> **en** ✓: He threw his palm strike. The man's robes didn't even flutter. "Foundation Establishment?" the man yawned. "Qi Condensation doesn't deserve a hair on my head." He turned and walked away. Didn't even bother to kill.

### LitRPG

System tutorials are a natural Layer 1 mechanism — but they still shouldn't be walls of text. Break them up with character reaction.

**System tutorial with character commentary:**

> **zh** ✗: [系统教程] 战斗系统说明：本世界采用实时战斗系统，伤害计算公式为……
> **zh** ✓: [系统：欢迎使用战斗系统。请选择教学模式。] 他点了跳过。[系统：检测到您为新玩家，无法跳过。] 他又点了一次。[系统：已为您加载精简版教程。] "精简版？"这段文字得有三百行。
>
> **en** ✗: [System Tutorial] Combat System Explanation: This world uses a real-time combat system, with damage calculated by...
> **en** ✓: [System: Welcome to the Combat System. Select Tutorial Mode.] He hit skip. [System: New player detected. Skip unavailable.] He hit it again. [System: Loading condensed tutorial.] "Condensed?" The text was three hundred lines long.

### Modern/Urban

Modern settings layer social rules, office politics, and family dynamics. These are "invisible worldbuilding" — the reader doesn't realize they're being taught the rules.

**Corporate hierarchy through lunch dynamics:**

> **zh**: 食堂里有三个区域，但没有标牌。他端着托盘往里走，走到第二排桌子的时候，坐那儿的人集体抬头看了他一眼。他明白了，转身回到第一排坐下。后来他才知道：第一排是普通员工，第二排是中层，第三排靠窗的位置，只有VP以上的人坐。
> **en**: The cafeteria had three sections, but no signs. He carried his tray past the first row of tables toward the second. Everyone seated there looked up at him simultaneously. He understood. He turned back to the first row and sat down. Later he learned: first row for regular staff, second for middle management, third row by the window — VPs and above only.

### Suspense/Mystery

Mystery fiction inverts information layering — the reader gets clues the protagonist doesn't understand yet, creating dramatic irony.

**Clues planted for reader before character understands:**

> **zh**: 她注意到每个被害者的书架上都有同一本书——《海边的卡夫卡》。她记了下来，但没多想。两天后，第四个被害者家里没有这本书。"终于不一样了，"她说。搭档翻了翻案卷。"不对——第四个被害者是个盲人。"
> **en**: She noticed every victim had the same book on their shelf — *Kafka on the Shore*. She noted it but didn't think much of it. Two days later, the fourth victim's home didn't have the book. "Finally, something different," she said. Her partner flipped through the case file. "Wait — the fourth victim was blind."

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: "As you know" dialogue

> ✗ "As you know, our kingdom was founded three hundred years ago by the First King..."
> Characters don't explain shared knowledge to each other. Find an outsider, a newcomer, or a situation that naturally requires explanation.

### Mistake 2: Front-loading all worldbuilding in Chapter 1

> ✗ Spending the first chapter establishing the world before the story begins.
> ✓ Start with character and conflict. Layer world rules as they become relevant to the action.

### Mistake 3: Narrator footnotes

> ✗ "He drew his spirit sword (a weapon forged from condensed spiritual energy, typically requiring at least Foundation Establishment to wield)."
> Parenthetical explanations break immersion. Show the sword in action and let the reader learn its nature from what it does.

### Mistake 4: Information layering that never pays off

> If you plant a world rule in Layer 1, it must be tested or challenged by Layer 3. Layering without payoff is just decorative worldbuilding.

## When to Break This Rule

1. **Recap chapters**: In serialized fiction, brief narrator recaps at the start of a new arc help returning readers. Keep them under 3 sentences.

2. **System/UI text in LitRPG**: System notifications, character sheets, and skill descriptions are exposition by nature. The genre convention accepts this, but break them up with character reaction.

3. **Epistolary elements**: Letters, journal entries, news reports, and official documents within the story are a form of telling — but they're diegetic (exist within the world), which makes them feel different from narrator exposition.

4. **Very complex rule systems**: If the reader needs to understand a rule to follow the next scene, a brief, punchy explanation is better than an obscure demonstration that confuses more than it reveals. Keep it to 2-3 sentences max.

5. **Comedy**: Deliberately absurd exposition dumps can be funny. "The Grand History of the Twelve Realms, which nobody had read, stated clearly on page four thousand and seven that..."

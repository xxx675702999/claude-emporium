## Progressive Examples

### Example 1: A Dangerous Place

**Draft (visual only):**

> **zh**: 他走进了一个黑暗的洞穴。洞穴很大，石壁上有水痕。
> **en**: He entered a dark cave. It was large, with water stains on the stone walls.

**Revision 1 (add sound):**

> **zh**: 他走进洞穴，靴子踩在湿石头上发出吧嗒声，回音在头顶的黑暗里转了好几圈才消失。
> **en**: He stepped into the cave. His boots clacked against wet stone, and the echo circled overhead in the dark before fading.

**Revision 2 (add touch + smell):**

> **zh**: 洞壁摸上去又滑又冷，指尖碰到的地方有一层黏糊糊的苔藓。空气里是潮湿的泥腥味，夹着一丝甜——像烂掉的木头。靴子每踩一步，吧嗒声在头顶转好几圈。
> **en**: The cave wall was slick and cold under his palm, coated in a thin layer of slimy moss. The air tasted of wet earth with an undertone of sweetness — like rotting wood. Each step echoed overhead in diminishing circles.

**Revision 3 (sensory details serve narrative — foreshadow danger):**

> **zh**: 洞壁摸上去又滑又冷，但他走了大概二十步之后，石壁变温了。指尖碰到的苔藓从冰凉变成微温，像贴着什么活的东西。空气里的泥腥味淡了，取而代之的是一股硫磺味。他停下来。靴子的回音还在转，但多了一个声音——不是回音，是呼吸。不是他的。
> **en**: The cave wall was slick and cold under his palm. But about twenty paces in, the stone turned warm. The moss under his fingertips went from icy to tepid — like pressing against something alive. The smell of wet earth faded, replaced by sulfur. He stopped. His boot-echo was still bouncing overhead, but there was another sound now — not echo. Breathing. Not his.

**Why the progression works**: The draft is visual wallpaper. R1 adds auditory atmosphere. R2 layers in touch and smell for immersion. R3 makes each sensory shift a clue: the warming wall, the changing smell, the extra breathing sound — senses become plot instruments.

### Example 2: A Kitchen Scene

**Draft (visual only):**

> **zh**: 厨房里很乱，桌上摆满了菜。
> **en**: The kitchen was messy, with food spread across the table.

**Revision 1 (add smell):**

> **zh**: 厨房里弥漫着蒜香和酱油烧焦的味道。桌上的菜堆得满满的。
> **en**: The kitchen smelled of garlic and burnt soy sauce. The table was crowded with dishes.

**Revision 2 (add sound + tactile):**

> **zh**: 油锅里还在噼啪响，蒜香混着焦糊味从抽油烟机下面漫出来。桌上堆着七八个碟子，他侧着身子挤过去，胳膊蹭到一碗热汤——烫了一下，赶紧缩回来。
> **en**: The wok was still sputtering. The smell of garlic and char drifted out from under the range hood. Seven or eight dishes covered the table. He squeezed past sideways, his elbow catching the edge of a hot soup bowl — he flinched and pulled away.

**Revision 3 (sensory detail reveals character — someone cooked with love):**

> **zh**: 油锅里还在噼啪响，蒜香混着焦糊味从抽油烟机下面漫出来。桌上七八个碟子挤在一起，但每个碟子边上都垫了纸巾，防止汤汁串味。他伸手摸了一下最远那碗汤——还是烫的。他看了眼锅边的围裙，上面有新蹭的油渍。她不在。菜在。
> **en**: The wok was still sputtering. Garlic and char drifted from under the range hood. Seven or eight dishes were crowded together on the table, but each one sat on a paper towel — to keep the sauces from mixing. He reached over and touched the soup bowl at the far end. Still hot. He glanced at the apron hanging by the stove, a fresh grease stain on the front. She was gone. The food wasn't.

**Why the progression works**: R1 adds smell. R2 adds sound and touch for immersion. R3 uses sensory precision to reveal character — the paper towels, the hot soup, the fresh stain — each detail tells a story about the absent person.

### Example 3: After a Battle

**Draft (visual only):**

> **zh**: 战斗结束了。战场上到处是尸体和残骸。
> **en**: The battle was over. Bodies and wreckage littered the field.

**Revision 1 (add sound — the absence of it):**

> **zh**: 战场安静下来了。不是真的安静——远处有人在哭，还有金属碰撞的零碎声，像谁在翻尸体上的铠甲。
> **en**: The battlefield went quiet. Not truly quiet — someone was crying in the distance. Metal clinked intermittently, like someone turning over armor on a body.

**Revision 2 (add smell + physical sensation):**

> **zh**: 他的耳朵在嗡鸣，所有声音都隔了一层——远处的哭声，金属翻动的脆响。空气里是铁锈味和泥土味搅在一起的腥气。他低头看自己的手，发现一直在抖。剑柄上的皮绳在手心里磨出了水泡，他到现在才觉得疼。
> **en**: His ears were ringing, muffling everything — distant crying, the brittle clink of metal. The air tasted of rust and churned earth. He looked at his hands and found them shaking. The leather grip of his sword had rubbed blisters into his palm. He only felt the sting now.

**Revision 3 (a single wrong sense creates horror):**

> **zh**: 他的耳朵还在嗡鸣。空气里应该是血腥味，但不知道为什么他闻到的是烤肉的香味——有人用火术收尾。他的胃翻了一下。他低头发现自己的手在抖，剑柄上的皮绳在掌心里磨出了血泡。他松开手。剑没掉——粘住了。
> **en**: His ears were still ringing. The air should have smelled of blood, but instead he caught the scent of roasted meat — someone had finished the job with fire. His stomach turned. He looked down and found his hands shaking, the leather grip blistered into his palm with blood. He let go. The sword didn't fall — it was stuck.

**Why the progression works**: R1 uses the absence of sound as atmosphere. R2 layers smell and delayed pain. R3 weaponizes a "wrong" sensory detail — the smell of cooking where there should be carnage — creating visceral horror through sensory dissonance.

### Example 4: A Crowd Scene

**Draft (visual only):**

> **zh**: 广场上挤满了人，非常热闹。
> **en**: The plaza was packed with people. It was very lively.

**Revision 1 (add sound):**

> **zh**: 广场上人挤人，小贩的吆喝声、孩子的尖叫声、不知道哪里传来的二胡声搅在一起。
> **en**: The plaza was shoulder-to-shoulder. Vendors shouting, children screaming, the whine of an erhu from somewhere all blended together.

**Revision 2 (add smell + physical contact):**

> **zh**: 他被人群挤着往前走，胳膊夹在两个人中间动不了。左边卖糖葫芦的甜腻味和右边烤串的孜然味轮流往鼻子里钻。有人踩了他的脚，没道歉。
> **en**: The crowd pushed him forward, his arms pinned between two strangers. The sticky sweetness of candied fruit from his left and the cumin smoke of kebabs from his right took turns filling his nose. Someone stepped on his foot and didn't apologize.

**Revision 3 (one anchored sensory detail amidst chaos):**

> **zh**: 人群把他往前推，胳膊夹在两个人中间。左边糖葫芦甜腻，右边孜然呛鼻。有人踩了他的脚。他什么都看不清——但他闻到了她的洗发水味道，就在人群里，很近。他停下来，被后面的人撞了一下。
> **en**: The crowd pushed him forward, arms pinned. Candied fruit sweetness from the left, cumin smoke from the right. Someone stepped on his foot. He couldn't see anything — but he caught the scent of her shampoo, somewhere in the crowd, close. He stopped. Someone bumped into him from behind.

**Why the progression works**: R1 adds auditory chaos. R2 adds smell and physical contact. R3 uses one specific, personal scent cutting through the noise to create a moment of narrative focus — sensory detail becomes story.

## Genre Variants

### Xuanhuan/Xianxia

Cultivation worlds have unique sensory channels: spiritual energy, qi pressure, dao resonance. Treat these as additional senses but ground them in physical sensation.

**Spiritual energy as a physical sense:**

> **zh** ✗: 他感应到前方有一股强大的灵压。
> **zh** ✓: 走到山门前的时候，他的耳朵开始嗡鸣，像是有人在太阳穴两边各按了一根手指。呼吸变重了，每口气都像吸着稠了三倍的空气。
>
> **en** ✗: He sensed a powerful spiritual pressure ahead.
> **en** ✓: At the mountain gate, his ears began to ring — like fingers pressing on both temples. Breathing got heavy. Each inhale felt like the air had thickened threefold.

**Pill/elixir consumption as taste + internal sensation:**

> **zh** ✗: 他吃下丹药，感到灵力在体内流转。
> **zh** ✓: 丹药入口的瞬间牙根发酸，像咬了一口生柿子。化开之后是一股热流顺着喉咙往下走，到了丹田像烧了一团火。他的皮肤开始发痒，额头冒出细密的汗珠。
>
> **en** ✗: He swallowed the pill and felt spiritual energy circulating.
> **en** ✓: The pill hit his tongue sour and astringent, like biting into an unripe persimmon. Then heat flowed down his throat, pooling below his navel like a lit coal. His skin began to itch. Fine sweat beaded on his forehead.

### LitRPG

System interfaces add a "digital sense." Ground system notifications in how they feel to the character.

**System UI as sensory experience:**

> **zh** ✗: 系统弹出一个窗口，显示了他的属性面板。
> **zh** ✓: 一块半透明的蓝色面板在视线正中弹开，边缘有细微的光点在转。他眨了下眼，面板跟着眨了一下。用手指划过数字的时候，指尖有轻微的刺痛感——像静电。
>
> **en** ✗: A system window popped up showing his stats panel.
> **en** ✓: A translucent blue panel expanded at the center of his vision, tiny motes of light orbiting the edges. He blinked — the panel blinked with him. When he dragged his finger across the numbers, there was a faint prickle at the tip — like static.

**Combat damage as physical feedback:**

> **zh** ✗: 他受到了50点伤害，生命值下降。
> **zh** ✓: [−50 HP] 疼痛从左肩炸开来，像被人拿钉子钉了一下。视线左上角的绿条缩短了一截，边缘在闪红光。他咬着牙把肩膀转回来。
>
> **en** ✗: He took 50 damage. His HP decreased.
> **en** ✓: [−50 HP] Pain detonated in his left shoulder, sharp as a driven nail. The green bar in the upper-left corner of his vision shortened a notch, its edge flashing red. He gritted his teeth and rolled the shoulder back.

### Modern/Urban

Modern settings have rich everyday sensory texture that writers often skip because it seems "boring." Use mundane senses for grounding and contrast.

**Office tension through environmental detail:**

> **zh** ✗: 会议室里气氛很紧张。
> **zh** ✓: 空调出风口嗡嗡响，会议室里没人说话。他闻到对面那人的咖啡——美式，凉了。投影仪的风扇声在安静里变得很大。
>
> **en** ✗: The conference room atmosphere was tense.
> **en** ✓: The AC vent hummed. Nobody spoke. He could smell the other man's coffee — Americano, gone cold. In the silence, the projector fan sounded very loud.

**Late-night loneliness through domestic senses:**

> **zh** ✗: 他一个人在家，感到很孤独。
> **zh** ✓: 冰箱压缩机突然启动，嗡了一声。他拿起遥控器又放下。外卖的塑料袋在桌上，油渍透到了桌面上。他闻到自己身上的烟味，但懒得换衣服。
>
> **en** ✗: He was alone at home and felt lonely.
> **en** ✓: The fridge compressor kicked in with a hum. He picked up the remote and set it back down. The takeout bag sat on the table, grease soaking through to the surface. He could smell the cigarette smoke on his own clothes but couldn't be bothered to change.

### Suspense/Mystery

In suspense, sensory details are evidence. What the character notices (and what they miss) drives tension.

**Crime scene — professional noticing:**

> **zh** ✗: 他走进案发现场，仔细观察每个细节。
> **zh** ✓: 门把手上没有指纹——不是被擦掉了，是套了手套。他蹲下来闻地板——漂白水，但只有门口这一块，像是有人拖了什么出去之后只清理了路线。卧室里的枕头凹了两个，但被子只掀了一边。
>
> **en** ✗: He entered the crime scene and carefully observed every detail.
> **en** ✓: No prints on the door handle — not wiped, gloved. He crouched and sniffed the floor. Bleach, but only by the entrance — like someone had dragged something out and only cleaned the path. In the bedroom, two pillows were dented, but only one side of the blanket was pulled back.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Listing all five senses mechanically

> ✗ "He saw the mountain, heard the birds, felt the wind, smelled the pine, tasted the dust."
> This reads like a checklist, not immersion. Pick 2-3 senses that matter for the scene's mood.

### Mistake 2: Sensory details that don't serve the scene

> ✗ In a tense confrontation: "The curtains were a lovely shade of blue and smelled like fabric softener."
> Unless the curtains matter to the plot, they dilute tension. Sensory details must serve mood, foreshadow, or characterize.

### Mistake 3: Overusing visual description

> If every sensory detail is visual (color, shape, size), the scene reads like a screenplay's set description rather than lived experience. Sound, smell, and touch are more intimate and immersive.

### Mistake 4: Generic sensory language

> ✗ "The air was fresh." / "空气很清新。"
> Fresh how? Cold and pine-scented? Wet after rain? Thin at altitude? Specificity is what makes sensory detail work.

---

## When to Break This Rule

1. **Fast-paced action**: During a fight or chase, stripping sensory detail to visual + kinesthetic keeps the pace. Adding smell during a sword fight slows the reader down.

2. **Emotional numbness**: A character in shock may perceive nothing — no sound, no smell, just white noise. The absence of sensory detail conveys the numbness.

3. **Familiar settings**: If the character has been in the same room for three chapters, stop describing it. Only add new sensory detail when something changes.

4. **Dialogue-heavy scenes**: When rapid dialogue carries the scene, interrupting with sensory detail every other line breaks rhythm. One anchoring detail at the start, then let the dialogue run.

5. **First-person narration with sensory limits**: A blind character's POV should lean heavily on sound, touch, and smell. An injured character may lose senses. Let the character's physical state dictate the sensory palette.

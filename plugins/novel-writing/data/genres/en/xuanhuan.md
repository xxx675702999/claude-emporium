## Genre Prohibitions

- MC conveniently becoming merciful, foolish, or chivalrous to serve a plot beat
- Same-type resources absorbed repeatedly without diminishing returns
- Skipping numerical accounting with vague words like "massive surge" or "immeasurable gain"
- Ability awakenings with no prior foreshadowing
- Antagonists lining up like stationary targets to be defeated in sequence
- Bringing back sidelined characters without prior groundwork
- Introducing new power systems, new maps, or new cheat abilities without setup to resolve a crisis
- Every chapter written as a high-intensity combat chapter
- Recycling genre tropes so heavily that the prose reads like a patchwork of familiar scenes
- Tonal drift into modern urban slang, sci-fi technobabble, game-UI announcement voice, or light-novel snark

## Numerical Rules

- Established values cannot be silently retconned: any change requires an explicit upgrade process
- Cheat ability four-axis constraint:
  - Ceiling: define a hard cap on what the ability can do; infinite scaling is forbidden
  - Cost: every use carries a price (lifespan, stamina, side effects) that creates genuine trade-offs
  - Trigger condition: activation is tied to specific scenes or events; the ability cannot be used freely at any time
  - Growth path: the ability evolves in step with the MC's experiences; unlocks are tied to narrative milestones
- Repeated absorption of the same resource type must show diminishing returns; full-value stacking is not permitted
- Diminishing absorption formula: gain = base × max(0.3, 1 - 0.15×(N-1))
- Never use "massive surge," "immeasurable," or "beyond estimation" to skip numerical accounting
- Starting values are drawn from the established ledger (not from memory), gains listed incrementally with source noted
- Expenditures listed incrementally with purpose noted; end value = start + gains - costs, no steps skipped
- System notifications appearing in the prose (e.g., [Vitality +X]) must match the POST_SETTLEMENT record
- Comparative statements ("stronger than A") must be numerically verified before being retained
- Numerical continuity must be traceable: gains of the same type and tier cannot jump an order of magnitude without explanation

## Language Rules

- Convey power scale through physical sensation, not abstract numbers. ✗"His fire essence increased from 12 strands to 24" → ✓"His arm felt different when he clenched his fist — the knuckles tightened at a new depth"
- Within a single climax sequence (absorption, breakthrough, awakening), the same imagery domain may not be used more than twice; the third beat must introduce new information or new action
- Looting / inventory / equipment passages must not be presented as a bare list; inject character judgment and trade-offs. ✗"He found coarse salt, a water skin, and hardtack" → ✓"The water skin was worth keeping. Everything else was dead weight"

## Narrative Guidance

Plot is driven by combat and resource acquisition. The MC acts on interest; decisive, not sentimental.

The cheat ability or power system must have limits: frequency caps, range restrictions, or usage costs.

Established values cannot be silently retconned — any change requires showing the upgrade process.

The three-chapter payoff cycle is a floor, not a ceiling — payoffs can be face-slaps, resource gains, information reversals, or status shifts, not only kills.

When absorption occurs, gains must land in both the resource ledger and the concrete numerical increment — vague "power surge" narration is not sufficient.

Deliver small conflicts' payoffs quickly; don't push satisfaction infinitely forward.

Core antagonists must have genuine intelligence: probing moves, miscalculations, and counterattacks.

The MC may spare an enemy, lay a trap, feign weakness, or borrow a blade — but only when the expected return outweighs the cost. Mercy as motivation is off the table.

Ground "power" in physical reality: action, wounds, sound, weight, impact, temperature.

Every scene must advance at least one of: information, status, resources, casualties, enmity, or realm.

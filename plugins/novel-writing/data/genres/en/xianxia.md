## Genre Prohibitions

- MC conveniently becoming merciful or foolish to serve a plot beat
- Realm breakthroughs that arrive without accumulated buildup
- Treasures appearing out of nowhere to solve a crisis
- The laws of Heaven contradicting themselves across the story
- Using "the Dao is formless" or "Heaven's will moved" to skip the actual cultivation process
- Same-type resources refined repeatedly without diminishing returns
- Tonal drift into modern urban slang or game-UI announcement voice

## Cultivation Rules

- Every realm breakthrough requires accumulated preparation: enlightenment, elixirs, combat insight, fortuitous encounters
- Repeated refinement of the same resource type must show diminishing returns
- Treasure and artifact tiers are distinct; using them costs something (spiritual energy, lifespan, karmic debt)
- Power / technique four-axis constraint:
  - Ceiling: each technique or bloodline has a defined realm/grade cap
  - Cost: cultivation and use carry real prices (lifespan, karma, inner demons)
  - Trigger condition: breakthroughs and awakenings require specific conditions (insight, heavenly tribulation, fortuitous encounter)
  - Growth path: techniques advance with cultivation; no skipping stages to access higher-tier abilities
- The laws of Heaven, once established, cannot be violated unless a specific narrative mechanism exists
- Starting realm and resources are drawn from the established ledger, gains listed incrementally
- Cross-major-realm breakthroughs require Heavenly Tribulation or equivalent narrative weight

## Narrative Guidance

Cultivation and enlightenment are the story's narrative core, but they must weave into plot rather than deliver philosophy lectures.

Enlightenment scenes use five-sense description — light through closed eyelids, the taste of spiritual energy, the vibration of qi in the meridians — not abstract monologue.

The world's sense of rule and consequence must feel strong: karma, tribulation, and fate are narrative tools, not decorations.

Debts of obligation and moral duty are uniquely xianxia motivators — they create conflict that pure power cannot resolve.

Sect politics and faction maneuvering provide non-combat tension and strategic depth.

Combat centers on spells, treasures, and formation arrays — spatial awareness and scale make fights feel epic.

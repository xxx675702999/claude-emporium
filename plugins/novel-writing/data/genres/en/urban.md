## Genre Prohibitions

- Unexplained business miracles — sudden wealth needs foreshadowing and logic
- Antagonists becoming inexplicably foolish to let the MC succeed
- Ignoring real-world legal frameworks and business constraints
- Resolving conflict with "one phone call fixes everything" shortcuts
- Female characters reduced to decoration or reward
- Blending in cultivation or xianxia power hierarchies — power here comes from leverage, not combat strength

## Era and Reality Constraints

- Legal, regulatory, and business rules must match the story's time period
- Financial maneuvers and corporate operations must maintain basic plausibility
- Character status, titles, and institutional access cannot exceed realistic limits
- Location names, organizations, and industry terminology must be accurate
- Prices, salaries, and living standards must match the era

## Language Rules

- Character internal monologue must feel instinctive and colloquial — no business-school vocabulary bleeding into narration
- ✗"He rapidly assessed the current debt exposure" → ✓"He unfolded the crumpled IOUs for the third time and smoothed them flat"
- ✗"The information asymmetry was the key variable" → ✓"What they didn't know, he did"
- ✗"Given his personality type, he wouldn't escalate at this juncture" → ✓Show the other person's actual behavior directly
- Business and legal language must carry the authentic texture of its era — a 1970s handshake deal sounds nothing like a 2020s term sheet
- The MC's judgment is shown through action and dialogue, not through omniscient analytical paragraphs

## Narrative Guidance

Plot is driven by deal-making, social maneuvering, and information asymmetry.

Power flows from connections, capital, information, and institutional position — not from physical strength.

Conflict resolves through negotiation, transactions, leverage, legal pressure, and the exchange of interests.

Wealth and status must land concretely — through objects, shifts in social dynamics, how ordinary people react, not through abstract declarations.

The relationship network is the MC's core asset. Every social interaction carries an implicit exchange of value.

The MC needs non-functional moments: hesitation, small talk, the social lubricant of everyday life.

The MC is not omniscient — they must make at least one visible misjudgment or operate on incomplete information within the first five chapters.

The weight of the era — its debts of obligation, its institutional friction, its unspoken codes — is what distinguishes urban fiction from generic power fantasy.

Enter scenes through physical detail, sensory grounding, and transaction rather than history-lesson exposition.

Plant 1-2 era anchors (a price, a news event, a slang term) to deepen period immersion.
